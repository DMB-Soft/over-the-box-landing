## Design Context

### Users
Hungarian small business owners — café, bakery, salon, restaurant operators. Non-technical, time-poor, often looking at this on a phone between customers. They want to understand what the product does and whether it's worth trying, in under 60 seconds. The job: decide if this loyalty card tool is right for their business.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, sharp, confident.**
Like talking to a smart friend who runs a coffee shop — knows business, speaks plainly, never condescends. Hungarian "te" form throughout. Outcomes over features, always.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: linear.app — minimal, sharp typography, smooth micro-interactions, purposeful whitespace, dark elegance with precision
- **Anti-references**: Enterprise/corporate (salesforce.com — formal, stiff, blue-gray), cheap/budget feel (cramped, clashing colors, banner-ad energy)
- **Theme**: Light mode only (for now). Warm neutrals (stone), not cold grays
- **Colors**: Warm indigo primary (#4F46E5), amber accent (#F59E0B), stone neutrals (#FAFAF9 bg, #1C1917 text). Amber evokes stamps and coffee. Indigo is distinctive vs competitors.
- **Typography**: Plus Jakarta Sans (headlines, 700), Inter (body, 400/500). Clean, modern, high readability.
- **Icons**: Lucide — consistent line style, 1.5-2px stroke

### Design Principles
1. **Earn every pixel** — No decorative filler. Every element serves comprehension, trust, or conversion. If a section doesn't pull its weight, cut it.
2. **Warm precision** — Linear's sharpness meets coffee-shop warmth. Tight spacing and clean type, but with amber accents and rounded corners that feel approachable.
3. **Obvious over clever** — The merchant should understand the value proposition in 5 seconds. No jargon, no abstract illustrations, no "clever" copy that requires interpretation.
4. **Motion with purpose** — Subtle scroll animations and hover states that guide attention, never distract. Always respect prefers-reduced-motion.
5. **Mobile-first honesty** — These merchants check things on their phone. Every layout decision starts at 375px. No desktop-only flourishes that break on mobile.
